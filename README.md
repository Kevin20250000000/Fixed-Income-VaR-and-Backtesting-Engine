# Fixed Income VaR & Backtesting Engine

An end-to-end quantitative risk management pipeline for fixed income portfolios. This engine simulates interest rate paths, prices underlying linear and non-linear instruments, computes key Risk Metrics (VaR/CVaR), and validates risk models under strict regulatory validation frameworks.

---

## 📌 Project Overview
This project orchestrates a comprehensive, enterprise-level risk management and model validation pipeline:
1. **Data Ingestion**: Simulated high-performance SQL pipeline fetching real-time positions and curves.
2. **Rate Model Initialisation**: Calibration of short-rate dynamics (Hull-White or Vasicek) to fit the initial term structure.
3. **Monte Carlo Simulation**: Full-revaluation P&L simulation across multiple paths to derive non-linear portfolio risk.
4. **Stress Testing**: Comprehensive evaluation across 6 distinct historical and hypothetical macro scenarios.
5. **Model Validation**: Regulatory backtesting and sensitivity analysis to detect structural model risk.
6. **Documentation Generation**: Automated report generation complying with regulatory expectations (e.g., SR 11-7 guidelines).

---

## 🧮 Model Framework
The engine implements short-rate models to generate forward interest rate paths and perform full portfolio repricing:
* **Vasicek Model**: Incorporates a strict mean-reversion process.
* **Hull-White Model**: Extends the Vasicek framework by introducing time-varying parameters to ensure a perfect calibration to the initial market term structure.

Unlike standard duration or convexity approximations, this engine utilizes a **Full-Revaluation** framework to accurately price complex portfolios under extreme market shifts.

---

## 🧪 Validation & Backtesting
To ensure model soundness under regulatory guidelines (e.g., Basel and Federal Reserve SR 11-7), the framework executes statistical validation tests:
* **Kupiec Proportion of Failures (POF) Test**: A likelihood-ratio test evaluating whether the frequency of historical exceptions matches the target significance level.
* **Christoffersen Independence Test**: Verifies if the exceptions are statistically independent or clustered in time.
* **Basel Traffic Light System**: Automatically classifies the performance into **Green**, **Yellow**, or **Red** zones based on the total number of exceptions.
* **Historical Simulation Benchmark**: Compares parametric and Monte Carlo results against a non-parametric baseline.
