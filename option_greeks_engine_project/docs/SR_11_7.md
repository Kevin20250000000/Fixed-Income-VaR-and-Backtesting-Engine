# SR 11-7 Compliance Guide

## 1. Purpose

This document describes the governance and validation framework for the option pricing and Greeks engine.

## 2. Scope

- Black-Scholes, Heston, Monte Carlo pricing
- Full-suite Greeks calculation
- Delta hedging simulations
- Stress testing and model risk reporting
- Backtesting and benchmark validation

## 3. Assumptions

- Market data sources are validated and governed.
- Model inputs are subject to approved calibration procedures.
- Pricing outputs are used only within approved limits and controls.

## 4. Limitations

- Black-Scholes assumes constant volatility and log-normal underlying dynamics.
- Heston calibration may be sensitive to low-liquidity options.
- Monte Carlo results are subject to simulation error and require convergence monitoring.

## 5. Validation Framework

- Backtesting using Kupiec POF, Christoffersen conditional coverage, Basel Traffic Light.
- Pricing bias benchmarks between Black-Scholes and Heston.
- Greeks sensitivity and parameter perturbation analysis.

## 6. Governance

- Model documentation, version control, and change management are maintained in the validation repository.
- Data lineage and source systems are recorded in the SQL/Python pipeline.
- Monthly review of model performance, stress scenarios, and parameter drift.
